Creating 3D human avatars with realistic expressions is difficult, despite the availability of many plugins to create and animate these avatars. In the context of virtual reality, this limitation can negatively impact the user's experience, as they are likely to notice the lack of realism in the avatars. To address this problem, the project in question focused on optimizing visual expression by improving the combination of facial rigging and body rigging. Facial rigging involves the process of adding control points to a 3D model's face to allow for manipulation of facial features such as the mouth, eyes, and eyebrows. Body rigging involves the process of adding control points to the rest of the model's body to allow for movement and posing. The project also utilized techniques such as weight-painting and shapekeys to further improve the realism of the avatars' expressions. Weight-painting involves assigning different levels of influence to the control points based on their proximity to various parts of the model, allowing for more natural movement. Shapekeys involve creating pre-defined shapes for specific facial expressions, allowing for more accurate and consistent reproduction of those expressions. Overall, the goal of the project was to create more realistic and expressive 3D human avatars for use in virtual reality environments, improving the overall user experience for emotional regulation.
